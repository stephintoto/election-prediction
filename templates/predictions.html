Understanding the Graph

This graph displays the predicted electoral votes for both Harris and Trump as the trust in polling data increases. The X-axis shows the polling trust level, while the Y-axis represents the projected electoral votes for each candidate.

Understanding the Graph

The error bars accompanying each data point reflect the uncertainty in the prediction. Wider error bars indicate higher uncertainty in the forecast, while narrower bars suggest more confidence in the model's predictions at higher trust levels.

Understanding the Graph

Hover over any point on the graph to view the exact predicted electoral votes for Harris and Trump at different polling trust levels. This helps visualize how changes in the weight of polling data affect the election prediction for both candidates.

Understanding the Graph

As the polling trust level increases, the uncertainty in the predictions reduces, and the race between the two candidates becomes closer. This trend highlights the importance of current polling data in refining the model's prediction accuracy.
